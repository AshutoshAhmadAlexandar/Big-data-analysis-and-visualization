Advertisement By KASIA PILATJAN. 5, 2018  Our guide to stand-up, improv and variety shows. ‘50 FIRST JOKES N.Y.C.’ at the Bell House (Jan. 5, 8 p.m.). At this event, now in its 12th year, 50 comedians share the first joke they’ve written since the start of 2018. The ebullient John F. O’Donnell will serve as host for the night, with a diverse roster of comedians slated to perform, including Ike Ufomadu, Janelle James, Jo Firestone and many, many more.718-643-6510, thebellhouseny.com YORAM BAUMAN, PH.D. at Caveat (Jan. 8, 7 p.m.). Billed as “the world’s first and only stand-up economist,” Mr. Bauman’s authority seems absolute: not only is he an author and educator, he also organizes the humor session at the annual meeting of the American Economic Association. With a take on comedy that deftly blends humor and academia, the audience is likely to learn as much as they laugh.212-228-2100, caveat.nyc ‘FRANK CONNIFF’S CARTOON DUMP’ at Q.E.D. (Jan. 10, 7:30 p.m.). Patton Oswalt is said to have once referred to Frank Conniff’s variety and stand-up show as being “like a kids show done in Bosnia.” Fans of the show “Mystery Science Theater 3000” will surely recognize Mr. Conniff, who hosts the event in character as Moodsy, the Clinically Depressed Owl. Styled as a kids’ show, but likely not appropriate for actual children, vintage cartoons are interspersed between comedy and music.347-451-3873, qedastoria.com ‘FUNNY AS TECH’ at the People’s Improv Theater (Jan. 9, 7 p.m.). Those who like their comedy on the nerdy side will find just that in the blend of tech and jokes that this show and podcast provides. Cryptocurrency like bitcoin and the blockchain technology underlying it are the topic at hand for this installment, with guests including the bitcoin comedian and entrepreneur Margaux Avedisian; the author and a founder of Placeholder Ventures, Chris Burniske; and the creator of the first artist cryptocurrency, Tatiana Moroz.212-563-7488, thepit-nyc.com ‘LAUGHTER IN THE CITY’ at the Brooklyn Museum (Jan. 6, 8:30 p.m.). For its inaugural “First Saturday” event of the new year, the Brooklyn Museum hosts a comedy show featuring Jordan Carlos of “The Nightly Show With Larry Wilmore”; the TED Fellow Negin Farsad; Ophira Eisenberg of NPR; and the “Saturday Night Live” writer Nimesh Patel. An open conversation, centered around humor and activism amid crisis, is to follow.917-727-523, laughterinthecity.org ‘REPEATER’ at Q.E.D. (Jan. 7, 8:30 p.m.). Once a month, the music fans Evan Forde Barden and Patrick Cartelli invite a guest to talk about one particular song they love and another guest to perform it.
